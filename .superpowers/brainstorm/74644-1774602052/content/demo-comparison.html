Three Demos at a Glance

Understanding what each product does and where INFER fits in

INFER (Your Project)

Multi-Agent Prediction Engine | ~21 min demo

What it does: Upload any document → builds a knowledge graph (GraphRAG) → generates 143+ AI agent personas with unique personalities → agents simulate social media reactions on Twitter/Reddit → produces a structured analytical report → interactive chat with any agent

Demo scenario: Financial market prediction (S&P 500, Brent crude, Iran-Hormuz crisis, NVIDIA earnings, Fed succession)

Key tech: GPT-4.1, GraphRAG, OASIS multi-agent framework, D3.js knowledge graph, ReACT report generation

Unique value: Simulates HOW people will react -- creates a parallel social world with hundreds of autonomous agents that debate, argue, and shift opinions

(In)Genius (Neha's Team)

Agentic LLM Research Platform | ~52 min demo

What it does: Multiple AI researcher agents (Agentic_LLM, Arabic_LLM, Health_and_wellness, g42_board, inception_board) each explore a knowledge graph, generate meta-reports with citations, evaluate findings across quality dimensions

Demo scenario: R&D research -- "Closing the Platform Capacity Gap", "Sovereign Clouds, Stronger AI", AI development strategy

Key tech: Knowledge graph exploration maps, multi-model evaluation (GPT-5, Azure-o3, Deepseek, Llama3p3 70B), LLM-as-a-judge scoring

Unique value: Automated strategic research with quality scoring -- generates 1500+ reports, evaluates hallucinations, measures quality across originality/coverage/relevance

Sentinel (Ricky's Project)

National Threat Monitoring System | ~3 min demo

What it does: Aggregates 2,245 intelligence items from 138 news sources → auto-classifies by domain/severity → visualizes threat knowledge graph → AI generates situational briefings with live web search → produces multi-phase strategic directives with agency-level task assignments

Demo scenario: Abu Dhabi Government -- Iran war threat monitoring, aviation disruption, maritime security

Key tech: RSS/OSINT aggregation, threat knowledge graph (events, campaigns, threat actors), AI impact analysis with web search, directive workflow (approve/reject)

Unique value: Signal-to-action pipeline -- goes from raw intelligence all the way to approved strategic directives with specific agency assignments and timelines

The Shared DNA

All three systems follow the same fundamental pattern:

Ingest Signals → Build Knowledge Graph → AI Analysis → Structured Output

The differentiation is in what happens in the middle: InGenius does automated research, Sentinel does threat classification + directives, and INFER does agent-based social simulation.
