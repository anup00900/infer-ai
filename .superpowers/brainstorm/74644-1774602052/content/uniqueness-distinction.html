What Makes Each Product Unique

Simple words, clear distinctions -- no jargon

All three products start the same way:

Feed it data → It builds a map of connections → AI thinks about it → You get a report

The magic is in how the AI thinks about it. That's where they split.

(In)Genius -- The Researcher

Answers: "What do we know about this?"

Imagine you hire 5 brilliant analysts. You give them a pile of documents and say "read everything and tell me what matters." They each go through the material, highlight important parts, connect the dots, and write you a well-organized report with proper citations.

That's InGenius. It reads, organizes, and summarizes what's already there. It doesn't make up new things -- it finds patterns in existing information and presents them clearly.

Simple example: You give it 100 research papers about AI. It tells you "here are the 5 biggest trends, here's who said what, here's what's proven and what's not." Smart librarian with perfect memory.

Sentinel -- The Security Guard

Answers: "What's dangerous and what should we do about it?"

Imagine you have a control room with 138 TV screens, each showing a different news channel. A team watches everything 24/7, flags anything dangerous, connects related threats on a big wall map, and writes action plans telling specific people exactly what to do and when.

That's Sentinel. It watches, classifies danger levels, connects threats together, and produces step-by-step orders -- "Navy do this in 24 hours, Foreign Ministry do that in 72 hours."

Simple example: Iran fires missiles. Sentinel instantly pulls 2,245 related news items, shows you which airports are affected, and generates a 3-phase response plan for the government. Alert board to action plan in minutes.

INFER -- The Crystal Ball

Answers: "What will people think, say, and do when this happens?"

Imagine you could clone 143 real people -- hedge fund managers, government officials, journalists, CEOs, ordinary citizens -- put them in a room, drop a bombshell news story, and watch what happens. Who panics? Who sees opportunity? Who argues with whom? How does the mood shift hour by hour?

That's INFER. It doesn't summarize existing information. It doesn't classify threats. It creates a parallel world of AI people who react, argue, and change their minds. The output is things nobody said yet -- predictions of what people will say.

Simple example: You upload a financial report. INFER creates 143 agents -- Goldman Sachs analysts, Fed officials, retail traders, journalists. They post on simulated Twitter. Bulls fight bears. After 3 hours of simulated debate, INFER tells you "65% chance markets drop, here's why, and here are the exact arguments people will make."

The One-Line Difference

InGenius tells you what the documents say  →  Looking backward at existing knowledge
Sentinel tells you what the threats are  →  Looking at the present situation
INFER tells you what people will do next  →  Looking forward into the future

What Makes INFER Truly Unique

  1. It creates new information, not just organizes existing info. The arguments between agents, the opinion shifts, the consensus that forms -- none of that existed in the original document. It's emergent.
  2. It simulates human behavior, not just data patterns. Each of the 143 agents has a personality (MBTI type), backstory, profession, biases, and memory. They don't just analyze -- they react like real people would.
  3. It shows you the "why" behind predictions. You can literally chat with any agent and ask "why did you say that?" and get a human-like explanation rooted in their persona and the simulated events.
  4. It models disagreement and debate. InGenius gives you one synthesized answer. INFER gives you the full spectrum -- what bulls think vs bears, hawks vs doves, insiders vs outsiders -- and lets the debate play out.
  5. It predicts sentiment cascades. Not just "positive or negative" but how opinions spread, who influences whom, and how the narrative evolves hour by hour on social media.
